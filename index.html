Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to common questions

How often should my pet have a wellness exam?

We recommend annual exams for adult pets and bi-annual exams for seniors (7+ years). Puppies and kittens need more frequent visits during their first year for vaccinations and growth monitoring.

What should I do in a pet emergency?

Call our emergency line at (555) 123-4567 immediately. Keep your pet calm and still. Do not give any medications unless instructed. Bring them in as soon as possible.

Do you offer payment plans?

Yes. We accept CareCredit, Scratchpay, and offer in-house payment plans for qualifying procedures. We also accept all major credit cards and pet insurance.

How do I prepare my pet for surgery?

Typically, no food after midnight the night before. Water is usually fine until morning. We'll provide specific pre-op instructions tailored to your pet's procedure.

What vaccinations does my puppy or kitten need?

Core vaccines include rabies, distemper, and parvovirus for dogs; rabies, FVRCP for cats. Lifestyle vaccines like Bordetella or FeLV may be recommended based on your pet's exposure risk.

Can I stay with my pet during the exam?

Absolutely! We encourage pet parents to be present during exams. For certain procedures, we may ask you to wait in our comfortable lobby, but we'll keep you informed every step of the way.
